Manjistha (Rubia cordifolia) Description

1. Brahmi Powder: Brahmi powder can be consumed orally by mixing it with water, milk, or honey. It is commonly used to enhance memory, improve concentration, and promote overall cognitive function. You can start with a small dosage and gradually increase it as per your requirement.

2. Brahmi Capsules or Tablets: Brahmi supplements are available in the form of capsules or tablets, which are convenient for consumption. These supplements are often standardized extracts of Brahmi, ensuring consistent potency. They are commonly used to improve memory, reduce stress, and alleviate symptoms of anxiety and depression.

3. Brahmi Oil: Brahmi oil is derived from Brahmi leaves and is used for external application to the scalp. Massaging the scalp with Brahmi oil helps improve blood circulation to the scalp, nourishes the hair roots, and promotes hair growth. It can also help in reducing dandruff and soothing scalp irritation.

4. Brahmi Tea: Brahmi leaves can be dried and brewed to make tea. Drinking Brahmi tea is believed to have calming effects on the mind and body, reduce stress and anxiety, and promote relaxation. It can be consumed daily or as needed for relaxation and mental clarity.

5. Brahmi Extract: Brahmi extract is a concentrated form of the herb and is available in liquid form. It can be mixed with water or juice and consumed orally. Brahmi extract is often used to improve cognitive function, enhance memory, and support overall brain health.

6. Brahmi Paste: Brahmi leaves can be ground into a paste and applied topically to the skin. Brahmi paste is believed to have cooling and anti-inflammatory properties and is used to treat skin conditions like eczema, psoriasis, and acne.
